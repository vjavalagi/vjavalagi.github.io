Who is Veda?

Hello, I am currently a Junior at Texas A&M University pursuing my Bachelor of Science in Computer Science. I chose to study computer science because I love to problem solve. However, coming to college, it felt isolating being in such a male-dominated field. I wanted to find a welcoming community on campus that also shared my interests, that's why I joined Aggie Women in Computer Science ( AWiCS ). I started as a freshman in the club and have held multiple roles over the years. Initially, I was a Workshops Officer my freshman year, the Secretary my sophomore year, and now I have the honor of serving as the Vice President. This organization has provided me with the opportunity to attend the Grace Hopper Conference this year. It is designed to celebrate women in computer science roles. Did you know that Grace Hopper recorded the " first computer bug found"? Funny enough, It was a moth found in the Harvard Mark II computer. I am very excited to attend this conference at the end of September and get to network with other professionals and attend technical workshops as well.

I also like to teach Computer Science concepts. The field gets a bad rap for being very hard to learn and challenging, but I believe a good teacher makes all the difference. That's why in high school I began running Intro to Java camps for middle school students because I didn't want other kids - especially young girls to have the same fears as me. I have taken this interest in teaching into college where I went to elementary schools with the Society of Women in Engineering to hold Engage Visits that teach middle school classes different STEM concepts. Additionally, I currently work for the Texas A&M Computer Science and Computer Engineering department as a Peer Teacher , where I hold office hours for previous courses I have completed. I also attend the CSCE 120, intro to C++ course, twice weekly to help students during their lab time.

I am very passionate about computer science and am curious by nature. I like learning new skills and I am open to work. Currently, I'm looking for a Summer 2024 internship in a SWE role. I have previously worked two internships in Houston at PROS and I can bring a lot to the table. Want to learn more? Click through this website to learn more about me!
